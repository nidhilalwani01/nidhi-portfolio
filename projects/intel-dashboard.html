Intel Sustainability Summit Dashboard

A real-time data dashboard designed for Intel's annual sustainability summit, visualizing environmental metrics, progress toward net-zero goals, and year-over-year performance across key sustainability pillars.

Role

UX Design Intern

Duration

4 months (2023)

Tools

Figma, Miro, Zeplin

Type

Web Dashboard (Desktop)

Intel Sustainability Summit Dashboard — Cover Image

Overview

Intel's annual Sustainability Summit brings together executives, partners, and stakeholders to review environmental performance and set new targets. I was brought on to design the central dashboard experience — a live data visualization tool used during the summit's keynote presentations and available to attendees on their devices during the event.

Problem

Previous summits relied on static PowerPoint slides to communicate performance data. This created several problems:

  • Data became stale the moment slides were prepared
  • Executives couldn't drill down into specifics when questions arose
  • Different teams had different versions of the data
  • Attendees had no way to explore data independently
  • Complex metrics were hard to grasp in slide format
Pain Points / Stakeholder Research

Goals

  • Design a live, interactive dashboard for real-time data display
  • Make complex environmental metrics immediately understandable
  • Support multiple user types: executives, analysts, and general attendees
  • Enable drill-down exploration without overwhelming primary views
  • Create a presentation mode for keynote use on large screens

Process

Stakeholder Interviews: I conducted interviews with 8 stakeholders including the Chief Sustainability Officer, data analysts, and event coordinators to understand their individual needs and mental models around the data.

Data Mapping: Worked with the data team to understand what metrics were available in real-time vs. processed data. Created a prioritization matrix to decide which KPIs should be primary, secondary, and exploratory.

Data Architecture & KPI Mapping

Dashboard Design Principles: Established four guiding principles — Clarity First, Progressive Disclosure, Context-Aware Comparisons, and Accessible Color Palettes. These drove every design decision.

Iterative Design: Created 3 distinct dashboard layouts and tested them with stakeholders in concept testing sessions. The winning direction used a card-based modular system that could adapt to different screen sizes and presentation contexts.

Outcome

  • Used live at the 2023 Intel Sustainability Summit with 500+ attendees
  • Stakeholder satisfaction score: 4.8 / 5
  • Executive team used drill-down features 23 times during Q&A
  • Dashboard adopted as the standard reporting tool for quarterly sustainability reviews
  • Reduced data preparation time for future summits by 60%

Reflection

Dashboard design is a unique challenge because the audience is often experts who know the data deeply but need it displayed in ways that aid decision-making under pressure. Designing for the "aha moment" — when a pattern becomes obvious at a glance — was the central design challenge of this project.
