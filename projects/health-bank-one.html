Health Bank One

A comprehensive UX redesign of a health data management platform aimed at improving patient engagement, streamlining data access, and building trust between patients and their healthcare providers.

Role

Product Design Intern

Duration

6 months (2022–2023)

Tools

Figma, Miro, UserTesting

Type

Mobile + Web

Health Bank One — Cover Image

Overview

Health Bank One is a health data management platform that helps patients store, organize, and share their personal health records with providers. When I joined as a design intern, the platform faced significant usability challenges — patients found it confusing to navigate, and providers struggled to access the information they needed quickly.

My task was to lead a full UX redesign: from discovery research to high-fidelity prototypes ready for developer handoff.

Problem

User interviews and analytics revealed several critical pain points:

  • Patients felt overwhelmed by the amount of information on screen
  • Finding specific health records required too many steps (avg. 7 taps)
  • Medical terminology was not explained, causing anxiety and confusion
  • Trust signals were absent — users weren't sure their data was secure
  • The mobile experience was not optimized for one-handed use
Problem Space / Research Findings

Goals

We defined three primary design goals:

  • Simplify navigation — reduce time-to-task by at least 40%
  • Build trust — communicate security and data ownership clearly
  • Improve comprehension — make health data legible for non-experts

Process

I followed a double-diamond design process, spending equal time in discovery and delivery phases.

Research: Conducted 12 semi-structured interviews with patients (ages 24–65) and 6 with healthcare providers. Synthesized findings using affinity mapping in Miro to identify key themes.

Affinity Map / Research Synthesis

Define: Created personas, journey maps, and a prioritized problem statement to align the team.

Ideate: Led two design studio workshops with the product team. Sketched 40+ concepts before narrowing to 3 strong directions for prototyping.

Sketches & Wireframes

Prototype & Test: Built mid-fi wireframes in Figma, ran 3 rounds of usability testing with 5 participants each. Iterated based on findings before producing high-fidelity designs.

High-Fidelity Screens

Outcome

The redesign shipped in Q1 2023 with measurable results:

  • Time-to-task reduced by 52% in post-launch usability testing
  • Patient satisfaction score improved from 3.1 → 4.4 out of 5
  • Support tickets related to navigation dropped by 38%
  • Mobile session duration increased by 27%

The project also resulted in a new design system adopted by the broader engineering team for consistency across all Health Bank One products.

Reflection

This project taught me the value of involving users early and often. Some of our initial assumptions were proven wrong in the first round of testing — a reminder that great design is never about what we think, but about what users actually need.
