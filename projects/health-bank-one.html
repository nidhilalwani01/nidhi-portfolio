Overview

Health Bank One is a healthcare platform concept focused on helping women with chronic conditions better manage their records, appointments, and care decisions. The project combined product thinking and interface design to create a more trustworthy, understandable, and actionable digital health experience.

Problem

Users managing chronic conditions often navigate fragmented health information, unclear terminology, and stressful decision points. Existing interfaces can feel overwhelming, which lowers confidence and makes record management harder over time.

Goals

  • Improve clarity of health records and care history.
  • Design interactions that communicate emotional trust and reassurance.
  • Reduce friction in recurring management tasks.
  • Support informed decisions with understandable, structured information.

Research Insights

Mixed-methods research combined qualitative interviews and synthesized feedback from target users to identify practical and emotional barriers in daily care management.

  • Users needed one place for records, updates, and follow-up actions.
  • Medical wording and data-heavy screens reduced confidence.
  • Trust increased when interfaces used plain language and clear hierarchy.
  • Progress cues helped users feel in control of ongoing care tasks.

Design Process

  • Discover: Defined user needs, constraints, and success criteria.
  • Define: Prioritized key workflows for records and care coordination.
  • Design: Built wireframes and high-fidelity prototype paths in Figma.
  • Validate: Tested for usability, comprehension, and trust cues.

Screenshots and Mockups

Wireframes mapped the core information architecture first, then evolved into high-fidelity Figma prototypes to test content hierarchy, navigation flow, and emotional tone.

Key UX Decisions

  • Used plain-language labels to reduce interpretation effort.
  • Grouped related records into predictable, scannable sections.
  • Added progress and status cues for recurring health tasks.
  • Applied calmer visual hierarchy to reinforce trust and reduce stress.

Final Outcome

The final concept delivered a structured, trust-focused healthcare experience with clearer records, simplified decision pathways, and improved usability across core management tasks. The work demonstrated how UX and UI strategy can make complex healthcare information more supportive and actionable.

Reflections

  • Designing for healthcare requires balancing precision with emotional care.
  • User trust must be designed intentionally through language and hierarchy.
  • Early research alignment reduced rework during prototyping stages.
  • A richer next iteration would include broader usability testing across diverse chronic condition journeys.
