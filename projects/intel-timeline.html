Overview

This project presents Intel's sustainability progress through an interactive timeline experience. The page was designed to make milestone data easier to scan, compare, and understand while maintaining a clean visual rhythm for long form storytelling.

Problem

Sustainability information is often dense and difficult to browse across devices. Without clear structure, users can miss important environmental milestones or struggle to understand progress over time.

Goals

  • Create a timeline format that is easy to scan and navigate.
  • Maintain readable content hierarchy for data-rich storytelling.
  • Ensure accessibility and usability across interaction patterns.
  • Prepare layout behavior for right-to-left language contexts.

Design and Development Process

  • Content Structuring: Grouped milestones into a timeline sequence with consistent entry patterns.
  • Layout Planning: Built a three-column structured section to balance chronology, context, and supporting details.
  • Front-End Build: Implemented semantic HTML and responsive CSS breakpoints for desktop and mobile.
  • Validation: Reviewed readability, interaction clarity, and task completion through usability checks.

Accessibility Decisions

  • Used semantic HTML to improve structure and assistive technology support.
  • Added meaningful alt text for timeline visuals and illustrations.
  • Applied clear form labeling and predictable focus behavior.
  • Improved text/background contrast for better readability.
  • Validated usability to reduce interaction friction in key sections.

Localization and RTL Considerations

The layout was tested for browser translation and right-to-left adaptation. Key content blocks and alignment patterns were built to remain coherent when text direction changes, preserving hierarchy and reading flow in localized contexts.

Responsive Design Decisions

  • Used flexible grids to support desktop, tablet, and mobile widths.
  • Adjusted timeline spacing and card stacking for smaller screens.
  • Preserved visual hierarchy and scannability across breakpoints.
  • Kept interactive touch targets large enough for mobile usability.

Final Outcome

The final webpage communicates sustainability milestones in a clearer and more accessible format. The combination of structured content, responsive behavior, and localization-aware design produced a stronger user experience for diverse audiences and devices.

Reflections

  • Timeline storytelling is most effective when information chunks are predictable.
  • Accessibility improvements early in build reduce downstream revisions.
  • Localization planning should be part of layout strategy, not a post-launch patch.
  • Balancing visual polish with readability strengthens content credibility.
